How a Sohar Trading Company Saved 3 Hours Daily With Automation

Discover how eliminating manual data entry transformed operations and boosted ROI for a local GCC business.

The daily operations of a trading business often involve a hidden cost: manual data entry. For logistics coordinators and sales teams, hours are lost copying information from emails into spreadsheets, updating inventory systems, and matching invoices. This tedious busywork not only drains employee morale but also introduces human error, directly impacting the bottom line. When a document gets misplaced or a digit is mistyped, the entire supply chain experiences friction.

Consider a mid-sized trading company operating out of the bustling Sohar Freezone in Oman. Despite having a strong client base across the GCC and a reliable network of suppliers, their internal processes were painfully slow. Every morning, the logistics team faced a mountain of PDF invoices, shipping manifests, and order updates. Processing these documents required printing them out, manually keying the data into an outdated ERP system, and then emailing the relevant departments to confirm receipt.

This manual workflow was sustainable when order volumes were low, but as the company expanded its operations towards Muscat and Dubai, the cracks began to show. The team was spending an average of 3 hours every single day just doing manual data entry. This bottleneck delayed shipments, frustrated clients who demanded real-time updates, and forced employees to work overtime just to keep up with the paperwork.

What is the True Cost of Manual Data Entry?

The true cost of manual data entry is the massive loss of productivity and the high risk of human error. To stop losing money, you need to calculate the hours wasted and replace repetitive tasks with intelligent automation.

Let's break down the numbers for this Sohar-based company. The logistics team consisted of three people, each spending approximately one hour daily on data entry. That is 3 hours saved daily if the process is automated, equating to 15 hours weekly. If we estimate the average operational cost of an employee in this role at 10 OMR per hour, the company was bleeding 150 OMR every week. Annually, that amounts to roughly 7,800 OMR in lost productivity—funds that could have been reinvested into growth or marketing.

Beyond the direct financial cost, there was the issue of accuracy. Human beings are not designed to process hundreds of lines of data flawlessly day after day. A single mistyped container number or a misplaced decimal on a customs declaration could result in goods being held up at the port, incurring demurrage charges and damaging client trust. In the fast-paced logistics environment of Oman's Vision 2040 economic expansion, relying on error-prone manual processes is a severe competitive disadvantage.

How Can AI Automate Invoice Processing?

AI automates invoice processing by using Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to read documents, extract key data points, and push them directly into your accounting software. To eliminate manual typing, you need an AI tool configured to understand your specific document formats.

The solution for the trading company involved implementing a custom AI automation workflow tailored specifically to their needs. The first and most critical step was tackling the invoice processing bottleneck. Previously, extracting data from a complex supplier invoice took an employee up to 45 minutes of meticulous checking and cross-referencing. The new automated system completely revolutionized this process.

Here is how the automated workflow operates:

  1. Automatic Ingestion: Whenever an email arrives with an invoice attached, the system automatically detects the attachment and downloads it to a secure cloud folder.
  2. Intelligent Extraction: A custom-trained AI model reads the PDF, instantly identifying key information such as the invoice number, supplier name, date, line items, and total amounts. It can even process dual-language documents, accurately reading both English and Arabic text.
  3. Seamless Integration: The extracted data is automatically pushed into the company's ERP system via API, creating a new draft record without any human intervention.

The result? Processing a complex invoice went from taking 45 minutes to just 2 minutes of automated background work. The employee simply receives a notification that the draft is ready, reviews the data for accuracy, and clicks approve. This single automation instantly eliminated the most time-consuming task of their day.

How Does Automation Improve Client Communication?

Automation improves client communication by providing instant, accurate updates without requiring staff to manually draft emails or messages. To keep clients informed, you need an automated notification system linked to your core logistics database.

In addition to invoice processing, the company struggled with keeping clients updated on their order status. Clients would frequently call or send WhatsApp messages asking if their shipments had cleared customs or arrived at the warehouse in Muscat. Responding to these inquiries required staff to stop what they were doing, search the system, and reply manually.

The automation solution addressed this by integrating the ERP system directly with a secure WhatsApp Business API account. Now, whenever a shipment status changes in the database—for example, when goods arrive at the Sohar Freezone—the system automatically triggers a personalized WhatsApp message to the client. The message includes the order number, the new status, and the expected delivery date.

This proactive communication strategy dramatically reduced the number of inbound status inquiries, freeing up the customer service team to handle more complex issues. It also provided a much more professional and responsive experience for the clients, aligning perfectly with the high standards expected by businesses utilizing Omantel networks and modern GCC infrastructure.

By implementing these targeted automations, the trading company successfully eliminated 3 hours of daily manual work. They didn't need to hire more staff to handle their growth; instead, they empowered their existing team to do more meaningful work. This case study proves that you don't need to be a massive enterprise to benefit from AI. Even small to mid-sized businesses in Oman can achieve remarkable ROI by identifying their biggest bottlenecks and applying smart, custom automation.
