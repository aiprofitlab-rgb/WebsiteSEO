How it works

Three steps. The first one costs you nothing.

Step 1

I look

We sit together — your office or a call. I trace where hours and money leak: the unanswered messages, the unchased invoices, the report that arrives three weeks late.

Step 2

I build

One system for the biggest leak first. It connects to what you already use — WhatsApp, Excel, your invoicing tool. Your team keeps working the way they work.

Step 3

I stay

I train your team, watch the system run, and fix what breaks. You have my WhatsApp. Delivery in 14–30 days depending on complexity.

Why this won’t end up abandoned like the Excel sheet you built last year: nobody on your team has to type anything in. The systems read what your business already produces — the messages, orders and invoices that happen anyway. Tools die when they need feeding. These don’t need feeding.

The direction Oman is moving

AI is now national policy here.

Royal Decree 50/2026 established the Artificial Intelligence Special Zone in Muscat in April 2026, with incentives for companies that build and adopt AI. The direction is set from the top. What that means for a business owner is simpler than the headlines: the tools got cheap enough and the government wants you using them. The businesses that automate their daily operations now will be the ones the next five years reward.
